Face Touch Alert Wrist Band

Project Overview

The Automation of Thermal Screening in Public Places project aims to enhance public safety by preventing individuals from unknowingly touching their faces an action that can increase the risk of COVID-19 transmission. The project introduces an FTA wrist band, designed to alert users when they bring their hands close to their faces. The wristband utilizes an infrared (IR) sensor to detect facial proximity and trigger a buzzer alert, reminding the wearer to avoid touching their face.

This project was developed with the objective of reducing human error in preventing virus spread, particularly during pandemics. Through sensor-based automation, the wristband improves individual awareness and encourages better hygiene practices. The FTA wrist band is a practical solution for public health safety, especially in crowded areas where maintaining social distance is challenging. By providing a gentle reminder to avoid face touching, the wristband contributes to the overall effort of minimizing virus transmission and protecting public health.

Features & Functionality

  • Infrared (IR) Sensor Integration: Detects hand movements when approaching the face and sends signals to the microcontroller.

  • Arduino Mini Processing Unit: Manages sensor input and triggers the alert mechanism.

  • Buzzer Alert System: Provides an audible warning whenever hand movement towards the face is detected.

  • Compact and Wearable Design: The wristband is lightweight, ensuring comfort and usability for everyday wear.

  • 2032 Rechargeable Battery: Powers the device with a long battery life, making it suitable for continuous use.

  • LED Indicators: Provide visual confirmation that the system is working correctly.

Project Demonstration

  • The IR sensor detects a human face when a user moves their hand toward it.

  • The Arduino microcontroller processes the signal and checks predefined conditions.

  • If the movement is too close to the face, the system activates the buzzer, warning the user.

  • The LEDs light up, indicating that the alert has been triggered.

  • The system works seamlessly through simulation and hardware testing, proving effective in real-life scenarios.

  • The project can be adapted for other health-related applications beyond COVID-19 prevention.
