Smart Phone Charging Using Footstep Power Generator

Project Overview

This project focuses on harnessing renewable energy through footstep movement. With the increasing depletion of non-renewable energy sources and environmental concerns, the need for green energy solutions is more critical than ever. The Smart Phone Charger Using Footstep Power Generator aims to provide an innovative way to generate electricity simply by walking. The project utilizes piezoelectric sensors, which convert mechanical pressure from footsteps into electrical energy. This energy is then stored in a 12V rechargeable battery and can be used to charge mobile phones via a USB output. The system ensures efficient energy harvesting and demonstrates how public places like shopping malls, railway stations, and airports can benefit from this technology to provide free and sustainable charging stations.

Features & Functionality

  • Piezoelectric-Based Power Generation: Converts footstep pressure into electricity using piezoelectric sensors.

  • Microcontroller-Based Monitoring: An ATMEGA328P microcontroller is used to track and regulate energy storage.

  • Rechargeable Battery Storage: The generated electricity is stored in a 12V battery, allowing efficient power usage.

  • RFID-Based User Authentication: The system uses RFID verification to ensure that only authorized users can access the charging facility.

  • LCD Display: Displays real-time voltage and charge levels for monitoring.

  • USB Mobile Charging Point: Allows users to charge mobile devices directly.

  • Eco-Friendly & Cost-Effective: Offers an alternative renewable energy solution, reducing reliance on traditional power sources.

Project Demonstration

  • Piezoelectric tiles are installed beneath the platform, where users step on the system.

  • The footstep energy is converted into electrical voltage, which is then sent to the microcontroller-based monitoring circuit.

  • The charge stored in the battery is displayed on an LCD screen, showing energy levels.

  • Users authenticate themselves via an RFID card, ensuring controlled access.

  • The mobile device can be connected to a USB port, utilizing the stored power for charging.
